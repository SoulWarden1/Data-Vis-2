Coffee Culture in Melbourne

Melbourne is renowned for its vibrant café culture, with thousands of cafés spread across the metropolitan area. This visualisation explores café density, travel patterns, pricing, and how Melbourne compares with the rest of the world.

Cafés Across Melbourne

Exploring café density, ease of access and growth throughout Melbourne.

Cafés per 1000 People

Top 10 Suburbs by Cafés per 1000 People

Melbourne's cafés are heavily concentrated in inner-city suburbs, while outer suburban areas generally have fewer cafés per resident. Some extreme values are caused by suburbs with very low population density.


Average Distance to a Café by LGA

Distance Travelled to Cafés by LGA

Residents of outer suburban LGAs generally travel further to reach cafés than those in inner-city areas. This reflects the lower café density in outer suburbs, as well as the tendency for residents to travel to more central areas for work and leisure activities.

Population and Café Distribution

This scatter plot compares suburb population with café count. Colour intensity represents cafés per 1000 residents, highlighting areas that attract café activity beyond their resident population. Of note are inner-city suburbs with high café density relative to population, which hug the left side of the main group of points. All of the black dots on the far left of the plot are suburbs with very low population, such as industrial areas or airports, but have cafés that serve commuters and workers in those areas.

Cafés Over Time

Melbourne's café scene has steadily expanded over time, reflecting the city's strong coffee culture. Growth was stunted during the COVID-19 pandemic, though recent years show recovery and continued expansion.

Melbourne Compared to Other Cities

Comparing prices and coffee consumption internationally.

Prices of a Small Flat White

Global Coffee Consumption

While Melbourne coffee is relatively expensive compared to other Australian cities, it remains affordable globally. Consumption patterns also show Melbourne sitting around the middle of international coffee-drinking trends.

Mapping Melbourne's Cafés

Café locations cluster heavily around the CBD, major train lines, tram corridors, and high-density inner suburbs.

Café Travel Patterns

Peak Cafe Travel Times
